February 14, 2026 · Product

Signals, not surveillance

Hunny is built around one question families ask: “Should I be worried today?” We deliberately avoid cameras, constant location tracking, and diagnostic claims. The product summarizes patterns from conversation — mood, routine, engagement — and surfaces alerts only when something meaningfully shifts.

When nothing is off, you should see calm reassurance: everything looks normal. That design choice is as much ethics as UX: peace of mind without turning a loved one into a dashboard tile.

February 1, 2026 · Context

Why the caregiver market matters

Tens of millions of people in North America provide care across aging, children, neurodivergence, and mental health. Many are digitally active but still piece together texts, notes, and apps — a fragmented stack that increases guilt and uncertainty.

Hunny’s bet is that a single, conversation-first companion can reduce that friction while respecting dignity: fewer pings, clearer shared context for the whole care circle, and early signals instead of late crises.
